# B2B Cold Email Landing Page Copywriting Guidelines

These principles must guide every word written on the landing page.

---

## Core Principle: Match the Email, Then Convert

The visitor landed here from a cold email. They clicked because something in that email resonated. Your job is to:
1. Immediately confirm they're in the right place (match the email's hook/angle)
2. Give them enough evidence to trust the offer
3. Remove all friction from clicking the CTA

---

## Headline (H1) Formula

The best B2B landing page headlines follow one of these patterns:

**Outcome-first:**
> "[Specific outcome] for [specific audience]"
> Example: "Book 3x More Sales Meetings Without Cold Calling"

**Problem-to-solution:**
> "Stop [painful problem]. Start [desired outcome]."
> Example: "Stop Guessing Which Leads to Call. Start Closing the Ones Ready to Buy."

**Number-driven:**
> "[X]% more [metric] — without [common objection]"
> Example: "47% Higher Reply Rates — Without Writing a Single Email Manually"

**Avoid:**
- Generic: "The Platform Built for Modern Revenue Teams" (says nothing)
- Feature-focused: "AI-Powered Pipeline Intelligence" (so what?)
- Vague: "Grow Your Business Faster" (how?)

---

## Subheadline Formula

One sentence that answers: "How does it work?"

Structure: "[Product/Company] [mechanism] so that [target audience] can [achieve outcome]."

Example:
> "Acme analyzes your prospects' buying signals in real-time, so your reps always know who to call first — and why."

---

## CTA Button Copy

**Good:** Specific action words tied to the offer
- "Book My Free Demo"
- "See It In Action"
- "Get My Free Analysis"
- "Start Closing More Deals"

**Bad:** Generic and forgettable
- "Learn More"
- "Get Started"
- "Submit"
- "Click Here"

Always match the button text to the next step. If it opens a calendar, say "Book a Call". If it's a form, say "Get Instant Access".

---

## Section-by-Section Guidelines

### Hero Section
- H1: Outcome (what they get)
- H2/Subheadline: Mechanism (how it works in one sentence)
- Supporting line: Social proof number or trust statement ("Trusted by 500+ revenue teams")
- CTA: Specific action

### Problem Section
- Agitate the pain. Use their language.
- "If you're a [role] at a [company type], you probably deal with..."
- 3 bullet points, each one a specific pain they recognize
- Don't be dramatic — be accurate. If they recognize it, they lean in.

### Benefits Section
- Benefits, not features. Translate every feature into an outcome.
  - Feature: "AI lead scoring"
  - Benefit: "Know which leads are ready to buy — before your competitor calls them"
- Use icons + short headline + one-sentence explanation
- 3-4 benefits max. Don't overwhelm.

### Social Proof Section
- Real quotes beat star ratings
- Specificity beats vagueness: "We went from 8 demos/week to 23" beats "Great tool!"
- If no real quotes available: use company logos + a stats callout

### Final CTA Section
- Restate the main benefit in the headline
- Address the last objection ("No setup required", "Cancel anytime", "Results in 30 days")
- Repeat the CTA button — same text as the hero

---

## Tone Guidelines

**Always:**
- Write in second person ("you", "your team")
- Be specific over vague (numbers, outcomes, timeframes)
- Speak to the ICP's role and context
- Short sentences. Active voice.

**Never:**
- Use buzzwords without meaning: "cutting-edge", "revolutionary", "best-in-class", "game-changing"
- Start with "We" — the page is about them, not the company
- Use "Solutions" as a heading — too generic
- Oversell — one strong specific claim beats five weak ones

---

## Copy-Email Alignment

The landing page H1 should mirror or extend the email subject line's angle.

If the email subject was: "Saw your team is hiring 5 AEs — here's how to ramp them faster"
Then the landing page H1 should be: "Ramp New AEs 60% Faster — Without Burning Senior Rep Time"

Not: "The Sales Enablement Platform Trusted by High-Growth Teams" (disconnected)

Ask Taha for the email angle before writing the H1 if it wasn't provided upfront.
